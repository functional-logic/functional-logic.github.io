Tsukuba is located about 50 kilometers from the center of Tokyo. The most convenient way to reach the venue is to first go to Tsukuba Station (also known as Tsukuba Center), and then take a local bus to the campus of the University of Tokyo. You can use [Suica card](https://www.jreast.co.jp/en/multi/suica/) for payment in most public transportation in Japan.

## Reaching Japan

The nearest international airports are Tokyo/Narita and Tokyo/Haneda. You can reach Tsukuba within two hours
by public transportation (train or bus) from these airports.

If you plan to go for sightseeing in Kyoto/Osaka, you may also consider using Osaka/Kansai Airport.

## Getting to Tsukuba by Bus

There are direct bus connections from Tokyo/Narita Airport, Tokyo/Haneda Airport, and Tokyo Station
to Tsukuba Center.

| Departure      | Destination                               | Time     | Fare      |
|:--------------:|:-----------------------------------------:|:--------:|:---------:|
| Tokyo/Narita Airport | Tsukuba Center                            | 1h       | 2400 JPY  |
| Tokyo/Haneda Airport | Tsukuba Center                            | 2h       | 2000 JPY  |
| Tokyo Station  | Tsukuba Center <br> University of Tsukuba | 1h 20min | 1260 JPY  |

For attendees arriving at either of the airports, limousine buses bound for Tsukuba Center are available from both Narita Airport and Haneda Airport. Tickets can only be purchased on the day of travel by cash or Suica card. Note that you must purchase a ticket at the counter/machine; you cannot tap your Suica card directly on the bus in this case. For more departure details, please refer to the [Timetable](https://www.chibakotsu.co.jp/en/kousoku/pdf/tm_natts.pdf?241001) and [Official webpage](https://www.chibakotsu.co.jp/en/kousoku/natts.html) for Narita Airport, or the [Timetable](https://www.kantetsu.co.jp/cms/wp-content/themes/kr/pdf/bus/2023111infohaneda.pdf) and [Official webpage](https://www.kantetsu.co.jp/en/lang/expressbus/tsukuba-hnd) for Haneda Airport.

For attendees arriving at Tokyo Station by train, there is a direct limousine bus bound for Tsukuba Center (please refer to the [Timetable (Japanese only)](https://www.kantetsu.co.jp/cms/wp-content/themes/kr/pdf/bus/hw_time_tsukuba-tokyo.pdf) and [Official webpage](https://www.kantetsu.co.jp/en/lang/expressbus/tsukuba-tokyo)). You can purchase tickets and board the express bus at the [JR Expressway Bus Service counter](https://www.tokyostationcity.com/en/information/bus_taxi.html) near the Yaesu South Gate of Tokyo Station. Alternatively, you can use a Suica card to board directly without purchasing a ticket in advance.

## Reaching Tsukuba by Train

You can use trains from the airports to reach Tsukuba, but you must transfer trains several times. The train line [Tsukuba Express (TX)](https://www.mir.co.jp/en/) connects Akihabara Station in central Tokyo and Tsukuba Station in 45 minutes (rapid) or 54 minutes (semi-rapid). Use internet search to find the best route from your departure place.
You only have to tap your Suica card at the ticket gates to board a train. Refer to the [Station Information](https://www.mir.co.jp/en/howto/route_map/) for details.

## Getting to the campus

Local buses take you from Tsukuba Center Bus Pool (above Tsukuba Station) to the campus. The nearest bus stop to the venue is "Dai-Ichi Area Mae".

| Departure      | Destination        | Time     | Fare      |
|:--------------:|:---------------------:|:-------------:|:----:|
| Tsukuba Center | Dai-Ichi Area Mae | 10min ~ 20min | 310 JPY |

At Tsukuba Center Bus Pool, find the No.6 Bus Stop.
Take the "Tsukuba-Daigaku-Junkan" (Univ. Tsukuba loop line) bus ([map of Tsukuba Center, Japanese only](https://kantetsu.jorudan.biz/?p=d&m=a&b1=%E3%81%A4%E3%81%8F%E3%81%B0%E3%82%BB%E3%83%B3%E3%82%BF%E3%83%BC&v)). Refer to the [timetable](https://www.kantetsu.co.jp/cms/wp-content/themes/kr/pdf/bus/timetable_files/center/center06.pdf) for departure times from Tsukuba Center and the [map](https://www.tsukuba.ac.jp/en/about/campus-access/tsukuba-access/campusbus_route_en.pdf) for the bus route.

Board the bus through the rear door and exit through the front door. Attendees using a Suica card must tap their card on the sensor located next to the door when boarding and getting off the bus. Alternatively, attendees paying with cash must take a ticket when boarding and pay the fare when exiting. Refer to the "How to get on and off the bus" section in [this guide](https://www.kantetsu.co.jp/en/lang/bus/guide) for detailed instructions.

IMPORTANT: you should ring the bell to let the driver stops the bus at the next stop.
(The bus stop before "Dai-Ichi Area Mae" is "Daigaku Kaikan" or "Dai-San Area Mae", depending on your bus.)

You can use a taxi from Tsukuba Center to the venue. 

## The Venue: Laboratory of Advanced Research B building 

The venue is on the first floor of the 12-floor building called Laboratory of Advanced Research B ("Sougou Kenkyuutou B" in Japanese.)
